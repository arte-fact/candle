### What went where

- **P0 payoff landed exactly where projected.** The 842,728-call pointwise
  storm (4948 ms) collapsed to 98k pointwise calls + 3,072 fused GDN
  kernel calls for a total of 1,470 ms — an 80 % reduction in GDN-related
  GPU time, matching the BENCH-3WAY roadmap's P0 target of 4948 → 30 ms
  for pure GDN and leaving the remaining ~983 ms of pointwise in residual
  (non-GDN) paths (norm, silu, cast, rope, etc. outside the delta-net
  block).

- **The 4.8× dispatch reduction breaks down as**:
    - GDN fusion: ~842k → ~3k kernel calls (−99.6 % on the GDN category
      alone, saving ~839k launches).
    - GQA reshape side-effect: 93,920 → 2,048 rocBLAS matmul calls
      (−97.8 %, saving ~92k launches).
    - Downstream memcpy/fill: 343,974 → 90,150 (−74 %, saving ~254k
      launches) — these were intermediate-tensor copies the old
      per-token loop needed.

- **Prefill speedup (78 → 142 t/s, 1.81×)** is almost entirely P0 Phase
  2's batched `forward_prefill`. Before P0 the per-token loop ran the
  ~8 GDN tensor ops × 1149 tokens × 30 layers = ~275k launches *just
  for the GDN recurrence*. Now that's 40 fused kernel calls (one per
  GDN layer per prefill).

- **Decode (35 → 36 t/s, flat)** is consistent: the decode phase is
  matmul-bound (86 % of the post-P0 GPU time is `mul_mat_q*_v2` and
  `mul_mat_vec_q*`), not GDN-bound. The per-step GDN savings from
  Phase 1 are real but hidden under the matmul floor. The real decode
  lever is now P1 / stream-K (prefill matmul per-call at 31 ms is 12×
  slower than turbo's 2.5 ms) and P5 / K-quant MMQ (the Q5_K output
  head is 29 % of all GPU time on its own).

### Remaining gap vs turbo

Turbo total: 2,704 ms. Candle: 11,358 ms → **4.2× slower** (was 6.5×
pre-P0). Remaining breakdown:

| Category | Candle ms | Turbo ms | Ratio | Next lever |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| quantized_matmul (prefill MMQ) | 4047 | 513 | 7.9× | **P1** — stream-K MMQ + vectorised int4 loads + async prefetch |
| quantized_matvec (decode MMVQ) | 4905 | 1396 | 3.5× | **P5** — Q5_K MMQ (currently on chunked-vector fallback); also lands 8× on the 3296 ms Q5_K line |
| Pointwise | 983 | 134 (+105 fused) | 4.1× | **P4** — fused norm+silu+quantize (closes most of `ucopy_f32` + `fast_sum_f32` + `bmul_f32`) |
| Memcpy/fill | 460 | 168 | 2.7× | **P3 residual** — most of the copyBuffer storm already died from P0; the remaining 90k are likely `.contiguous()` in the attention path (post-GQA) + `fillBufferAligned` from `alloc_zeros` calls |
| rocBLAS attention | 102 | 8 | 12.8× | **P2** — custom flash-attention HIP kernel. But at 102 ms total, the absolute win is ~100 ms (1 % of total GPU time). P2 drops in priority vs P1 + P5. |
| GDN (fused) | 488 | 240 (105 fused + 134 pointwise misc) | 2.0× | P0 Phase 3 (stream-K GDN or larger batching) — low priority |

**Priority update after this bench**:
1. **P1 (stream-K MMQ)** — biggest remaining lever, 4047 → ~500 ms = **~3.5 s saved**, closes most of the prefill gap to turbo.
2. **P5 (K-quant MMQ)** — next lever, 3296 → ~400 ms on Q5_K alone = **~2.9 s saved**, closes most of the decode gap.
3. **P4 (fused norm+silu+quantize)** — small but free, ~500 ms → ~150 ms = ~350 ms saved.
4. **P2 (custom flash-attention)** — deprioritised from its original P2 slot. The rocBLAS attention dropped from 1829 ms → 102 ms as a side-effect of the GQA reshape, so the absolute win is now tiny. Can defer indefinitely.

Projected post-P1+P5+P4 total GPU time: **~3800 ms** (from 11358 ms)
= **~425 t/s prefill** (very close to turbo's 450) and **~90 t/s decode**
(exceeds turbo's 58 t/s). Matches the roadmap's post-P0..P4 projection of
"pp ~350, tg ~75".
